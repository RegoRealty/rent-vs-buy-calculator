Ontario: Rent vs. Buy Calculator

Compare the true cost of renting versus buying over time — with Ontario-specific taxes, HST on new builds & incentives

🏠 Buying Inputs
Ontario LTT: Calculated automatically. First-time buyers get up to $4,000 rebate.
CMHC Insurance: Required if down payment < 20%. Added to mortgage automatically.
Stress Test: Qualification rate = your rate + 2% or 5.25%, whichever is higher.
New Build HST: New homes are subject to 13% HST. Buyers may qualify for the NHRP - up to $6,300 federal (GST) + up to $24,000 provincial (Ontario) if price < $450K. Partial provincial rebate for $450K-$600K range. No rebate above $600K.
🏢 Renting Inputs
Ontario Guideline: The 2025 Ontario rent increase guideline is 2.5%. Landlords can only increase rent once every 12 months. Units first occupied after Nov 15, 2018 are exempt from guidelines.
FHSA: The First Home Savings Account allows first-time buyers to save up to $8,000/year (lifetime max $40,000) tax-free. Contributions are tax-deductible, and withdrawals for a qualifying home purchase are tax-free.
